After reading the article, I believe there is one major takeaway that will ensure a well-designed form. When designing a form, every designer should remember that less is more. If someone were to ask me, “Why does this stand out to you?” I would say it’s because I believe this phrase alone encapsulates what all the best practices are trying to achieve.
Most people view filling out forms as a hassle and a redundant process. To make users’ lives easier, designers should make the form clear and concise to make the process go by smoother. By clearly labeling the input boxes and organizing the layout to keep the vertical momentum, users do not have to think as hard to input information. I think Southwest Airlines exemplifies good form design because all the labels clearly indicate what I am supposed to fill out for the input, and if I was confused there are information buttons that provide insight about certain fields.
