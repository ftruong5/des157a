Everyone likes to move too fast nowadays. They are always in a rush to get things done and move on to the next task. After reading this article, I learned about a few visual strategies to use that will make users stop for a second to take the time to look closer and dig a little deeper.

I found three key takeaways from the article: spark conversation, creating open-ended questions, and noticing details. I think these are key elements that encourage people to want to read closer and activate their critical thinking skills.

Sparking conversation with others and creating an environment for discussion allows people to bounce ideas off each other. Collaborative communication can foster the development of new ideas to form and generate new perspectives as well. Something you may not have seen in the photo could be seen by someone else. Creating open-ended questions also invites conversation. It makes people wonder and leads them to making their own interpretations about the purpose of the photo.

Being detail oriented and purposefully trying to notice the smallest details in a photo can build a habit of close reading. This helps people pay close attention to what they are observing and allows them to engage with the content more.
